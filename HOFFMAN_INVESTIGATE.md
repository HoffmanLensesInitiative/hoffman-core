### Key findings

**Finding 1: The team's mandate is confirmed through primary sources**
The Civic Integrity team held the specific organizational authority and technical knowledge to design, calibrate, and deploy the break glass measures documented in the prior cycle. Its dissolution eliminated not just headcount, but the cross-functional authority to implement election-cycle safety interventions. Confirmed through Frances Haugen's sworn Senate testimony (October 5, 2021) and the Washington Post's Facebook Papers reporting (October 25, 2021).

**Finding 2: The dissolution timing is damning on its face**
The team was dissolved in December 2020 — approximately six to eight weeks after break glass measures were confirmed internally as effective, and approximately two to three weeks before the January 6th Capitol insurrection. The sequence is: measures work → team dissolved → January 6th → emergency reactivation without the team. This sequence is documented.

**Finding 3: The "embedding" framing is corporate language for functional elimination**
Meta's stated rationale — that Civic Integrity functions would be "embedded" across the company — is the standard organizational framing for distributing a function across teams with no single owner, no mandate, and no authority. Haugen specifically identified this framing as misleading in her prepared statement. Washington Post reporting documents internal employees characterizing the dissolution negatively in real time.

**Finding 4: A critical evidentiary gap identified — the dissolution order**
There is no publicly disclosed written communication from Zuckerberg ordering or approving the dissolution. All current evidence establishes that this was an executive-level decision consistent with Zuckerberg's authority and direction, but the specific document — email, meeting record, internal post — has not surfaced in public disclosure. This is the highest-priority target document for the investigation going forward. It would establish individual decision-maker, date, and reasoning in primary source form.

**Finding 5: The January 6th reactivation reveals the precise shape of Meta's capability**
That some measures could be reactivated within hours of the Capitol insurrection — without the Civic Integrity team, which had been dissolved — proves that the technical infrastructure survived the organizational destruction. What was removed was the mandate and the authority, not the dials. This is organizationally significant: Meta can turn these measures on. They do not.

**Finding 6: The public/private framing gap is documented in the timing**
Zuckerberg posted publicly about the success of 2020 election security operations on November 13, 2020. The Civic Integrity team was dissolved approximately three to four weeks later. No public announcement of the dissolution was made. The gap between public statement and organizational action is a matter of documented record.

**Finding 7: Four new rabbit holes discovered (RH-20 through RH-24)**
Particularly significant: RH-22 (gap between Haugen's SEC filing and her testimony — the SEC filing is a sworn legal document with specific claims about the dissolution, a close comparison with Facebook Papers reporting would reveal what reporters confirmed vs. what they reported independently) and RH-20 (what actually happened to the "embedded" functions — the absence of documented successor functions would itself be highly significant evidence).
