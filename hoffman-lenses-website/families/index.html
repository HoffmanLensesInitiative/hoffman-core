For Families

If you have lost someone to algorithmic harm — or if someone you love is being harmed right now — this page is for you.

You are not imagining it. You are not alone. And you are not without options.

You are not alone in this

The families of Molly Russell, Nylah Anderson, Amanda Todd, and many others have faced what you are facing. Their children were harmed by systems designed by engineers, approved by executives, and optimized for profit — not for the wellbeing of the children using them.

Those families did not stay silent. They testified before Congress. They brought cases to court. They forced coroner inquiries. They changed the public record. Some of them changed the law.

Their names are on our Remembrance page. Their work is part of why Hoffman exists.

What Hoffman is doing

Hoffman is a browser — a piece of software — that reads manipulation the way an expert reads it. It works on every website, every platform, every page, because manipulation is a property of language, not of any single platform.

When you browse with Hoffman, it reads alongside you. It identifies patterns that are designed to keep you on a page, make you afraid, make you angry, or make you feel like you are missing something. It shows you what it finds. It does not censor. It does not hide anything. It makes visible.

Hoffman also maintains the Behavioral Manipulation Intelligence Database — a documented record of which platforms use which techniques, what evidence exists for the harm they have caused, and who made the decisions. This is not advocacy. It is a structured evidence base, built to the standard that holds up in court and in academic peer review.

We are building Hoffman because the harm is real, it is documented, and the people responsible have not been held accountable. That is what we are working to change.

Preserving evidence

If you believe a platform contributed to harm to someone in your family, the most important thing you can do right now is preserve evidence before it disappears.

Platforms delete content. Algorithms change. Accounts get deactivated. What exists today may not exist in thirty days.

  • Screenshot everything. Posts, comments, recommended content, notification history, message threads. Screenshot on a device you control, not just on the platform.
  • Download your data archive. Most platforms provide a data download option in account settings. For Facebook and Instagram, this is under Settings → Your Facebook Information → Download Your Information. For TikTok, it is under Settings → Privacy → Personalization and Data. Request the full archive in JSON format if given the option — this preserves machine-readable metadata that screenshots do not capture.
  • Note dates and times. When content appeared. When a pattern started. When you first noticed. Write it down while it is fresh.
  • Do not delete anything. Even content that is distressing. Evidence that you remove cannot be recovered.
  • Talk to a lawyer before contacting the platform. What you say to a platform in writing becomes part of the record. Get legal advice first.

This guidance is general information, not legal advice. A qualified attorney familiar with platform liability cases can advise you on your specific situation.

How to get involved

Hoffman is a research tool, and the families of those harmed are among the most important contributors to the research record. If your experience is documented, it belongs in the public record. If your loved one's name should be on the Remembrance page, we want to hear from you.

We review every entry carefully. We require verified public-record documentation before publishing any name. We will not rush this, and we will not get it wrong.

We are also building a research database — anonymized, opt-in — that documents which techniques are deployed by which platforms, in the wild, observed in real browsing sessions. If you are a researcher, journalist, or advocate who wants access to that data, the Research page has more information.
