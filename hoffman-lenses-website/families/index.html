For Families

If you have lost someone to algorithmic harm — this page is for you.

What happened to your family was not an accident. It was not a mystery. It was foreseeable, documented, and in many cases known to the companies responsible before your loss occurred. This page exists to tell you what we know, what we are building, and how you can be part of holding that accountable.

What happened to your family was foreseeable

The platforms that shaped your loved one's experience were not neutral tools. They were designed — with enormous resources and sophisticated engineering — to maximize the time people spend on them. That objective, pursued without obligation to human wellbeing, is what behavioral manipulation systems do.

The companies behind these systems had access to their own internal research documenting the harm. In some cases that research was suppressed. In some cases executives received findings showing the harm was worsening and continued the practices anyway. In some cases they testified under oath about platform safety while internal documents told a different story.

This is on the public record. Court filings. Whistleblower disclosures. Coroner rulings. Congressional testimony transcripts. The Hoffman Lenses Initiative is building a structured, evidence-based database of all of it.

If you want to understand what the documented record shows, the white paper is the place to start: The Algorithm and the Child: A Human Rights Case for Abolishing Behavioral Manipulation Systems .

What the Hoffman Lenses Initiative is doing

We are building tools and research infrastructure designed to make manipulation visible and to document accountability. Here is what is being built:

  • The Hoffman Browser — A desktop browser that reads the text on every page you visit and tells you, in plain language, what manipulation techniques are present and who is deploying them. It runs entirely on your device. Nothing leaves your machine. It works on every platform because it reads rendered language, not platform code.
  • The Behavioral Manipulation Intelligence Database (BMID) — A structured, evidence-based database documenting the operators, techniques, business models, and documented harms of behavioral manipulation systems. Every entry requires primary source documentation: court filings, sworn testimony, internal documents disclosed through legal proceedings, named journalism. No speculation. No inference without documentation.
  • The white paper — A documented human rights case establishing that behavioral manipulation systems cause foreseeable harm and that the companies responsible had knowledge of that harm. Fully cited. Written to be useful to lawyers, researchers, journalists, and families.
  • The Remembrance page — A record of individuals whose deaths have been linked by coroner findings, court proceedings, or documented public record to algorithmic harm. Not statistics. People. Names, ages, who they were. Read it here.

How you can get involved

If your family has been affected by algorithmic harm, there are things you can do. The most important one, if you have not already done it, is to speak to a lawyer who specializes in this area. See the legal resources section below.

If you are willing to share your experience for research purposes, we want to hear from you. Documentation of real harm, from families who lived it, is part of the evidentiary record that makes accountability possible. Any communication with the Hoffman Lenses Initiative is treated with strict confidentiality. We will not share your information without your explicit permission.

If you have documentation — records of your loved one's device use, social media archives, communications with platforms, or legal proceedings — that documentation may be valuable. A lawyer can advise you on how to preserve and use it.

Legal resources

Litigation against the major social media platforms is active and growing. Families across the United States and internationally have filed lawsuits against Meta, TikTok, Snapchat, YouTube, and others. There are law firms that specialize in this work and take cases on behalf of families.

Social Media Victims Law Center

socialmediavictims.org

The Social Media Victims Law Center represents children and families harmed by social media platforms. They have filed cases against Meta (Facebook, Instagram), TikTok, Snapchat, YouTube, and others on behalf of families who have experienced suicide, self-harm, eating disorders, sexual exploitation, and other documented harms linked to platform use.

They offer free consultations. Many cases are taken on a contingency basis, meaning families do not pay unless there is a recovery. Their website includes resources for families, a case intake form, and information about pending litigation.

The Hoffman Lenses Initiative has no affiliation with the Social Media Victims Law Center. We list them because they represent families in this area and have a documented public record of doing so. We encourage you to do your own research and consult with legal counsel directly.

If you are outside the United States, laws and available legal remedies will differ by jurisdiction. The UK Molly Russell inquest (2022) established legal precedent regarding platform liability in England and Wales. EU law, including the Digital Services Act, creates additional obligations for large platforms operating in Europe. An attorney in your country can advise you on what applies in your situation.

The white paper

The Algorithm and the Child: A Human Rights Case for Abolishing Behavioral Manipulation Systems documents the design of behavioral manipulation systems, the evidence of known harm, and the legal framework for accountability under human rights law.

It is written to be readable by non-specialists. It is fully cited. It was written to be useful: to families seeking to understand what happened, to lawyers building cases, to journalists covering the story, and to researchers working on solutions.

The Hoffman Lenses project takes its name from the glasses in John Carpenter's 1988 film They Live — lenses that let the wearer see hidden messages embedded in ordinary reality. Once you put them on, you cannot unsee what they reveal.

That is what we are building. Not to expose the hidden messages so people can look away — but so they cannot be used against us, or our children, without our knowledge.

We are sorry for what your family has been through. You are not alone in this.
