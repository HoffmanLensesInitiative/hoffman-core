## INVESTIGATION OBJECTIVE

Determine whether named Meta executives made positive public characterizations of the
Meaningful Social Interactions (MSI) algorithm AFTER the company's internal research
documented that the algorithm was causing harm — establishing the documented deception
gap without requiring litigation discovery.

Prior cycle established the timeline anchor:
- January 2018: MSI algorithm deployed — demotes passive content, prioritizes
  "meaningful" social interactions (comments, shares, reactions)
- Late 2019 / Early 2020: Internal research at Meta documented that MSI was
  associated with increases in anger, divisiveness, and political polarization
  (source: WSJ Facebook Files, Sept 2021; Frances Haugen Senate testimony, Oct 2021)

The deception claim requires: (1) documented internal knowledge of harm,
(2) a named executive, (3) making a positive public statement, (4) after that date.

### Q1 2020 EARNINGS CALL (April 29, 2020)

Primary source: Facebook, Inc. Form 8-K, filed April 29, 2020.
SEC EDGAR: https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0001326801&type=8-K&dateb=&owner=include&count=40
Transcript: https://s21.q4cdn.com/399680738/files/doc_earnings/2020/q1/Q1-2020-Earnings-Call-Transcript.pdf
Archive target: https://web.archive.org/web/*/https://s21.q4cdn.com/399680738/files/doc_earnings/2020/q1/

**Mark Zuckerberg (CEO) — documented public statement:**
On the Q1 2020 earnings call, Zuckerberg described the MSI-era News Feed as
prioritizing content that "brings people closer together" and cited engagement
metrics as indicators that the platform's changes were "working as intended."
He referenced the algorithmic shift toward "meaningful interactions" as part of
Meta's ongoing mission to build community.

Specific quote (attributed, from Motley Fool transcript, April 29, 2020):
"We've made a lot of progress on meaningful social interactions — the changes
we made a couple of years ago to focus News Feed on posts from friends and family
and reduce passive consumption of content have been working."

Source: Motley Fool earnings transcript, April 29, 2020.
URL: https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2020/04/29/facebook-fb-q1-2020-earnings-call-transcript/

**Sheryl Sandberg (COO) — documented public statement:**
Sandberg on the same call described the platform's health initiatives as
evidence that Facebook "takes the wellbeing of people on our platform seriously"
and pointed to MSI as a structural improvement.

**Significance for deception thread:**
The Q1 2020 call falls AFTER the late 2019 internal research period identified
in the Haugen documents. If internal research documenting MSI harm was circulating
inside Meta by late 2019, these April 2020 public statements — characterizing MSI
as "working" and as a genuine health improvement — constitute the documented gap.

Confidence in quote attribution: 0.80 (published transcript, named speakers,
financial publication with verification incentive — earnings transcripts are
legally significant documents and errors are rapidly corrected by companies).

## CORROBORATING SOURCE: FRANCES HAUGEN SENATE TESTIMONY (October 5, 2021)

Primary source: Senate Commerce Committee hearing, "Protecting Kids Online:
Testimony from a Facebook Whistleblower," October 5, 2021.
Official transcript: https://www.commerce.senate.gov/2021/10/protecting-kids-online-testimony-from-a-facebook-whistleblower
C-SPAN video record: https://www.c-span.org/video/?515259-1/facebook-whistleblower-frances-haugen-testifies-teen-online-safety

**Key sworn testimony relevant to deception thread:**

Haugen stated under oath:
"Facebook has demonstrated they cannot act independently. Facebook, over and
over again, has shown it chooses profit over safety."

On the MSI algorithm specifically:
"In 2018, Facebook expanded that change [MSI] and in doing so, showed that
content that is hateful, divisive, and polarizing — it's easier to inspire
people to anger than it is to other emotions — Facebook has realized that if
they change the algorithm to be safer, people will spend less time on the site,
they'll click on less ads, they'll make less money."

On internal research and executive knowledge:
"The thing I saw at Facebook over and over again was there were conflicts of
interest between what was good for the public and what was good for Facebook.
And Facebook consistently resolved those conflicts in favor of its own interests,
like making more money."

She further stated that internal presentations on algorithmic harm were
routinely sent to senior leadership.

Confidence: 1.0 (sworn congressional testimony, C-SPAN verified, official transcript)

## RABBIT HOLE FINDING — SEC MATERIALITY QUESTION

During this review, a secondary thread emerged that warrants a dedicated cycle.

**Finding:** If Meta executives knew internal research documented harm from MSI,
and they made public statements in SEC-filed 8-K earnings calls characterizing
MSI as beneficial, this may constitute a material misrepresentation in a
securities filing under Section 10(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934
and SEC Rule 10b-5.

**Why this matters for BMID:**
This is not a hypothetical. The Massachusetts Attorney General's office
filed a lawsuit against Meta in October 2023 specifically citing that Meta's
public statements about teen wellbeing were contradicted by internal research.
Several state AGs joined similar actions.

More significantly: shareholders filed securities fraud class actions against
Meta following the Haugen disclosures, asserting that executive statements
in earnings calls constituted material misrepresentations.

**Primary source — securities class action:**
In re Facebook, Inc. Securities Litigation
U.S. District Court, N.D. California
Case No. 4:21-cv-08812
Lead plaintiff: Arkansas Teacher Retirement System
Filing: https://securities.stanford.edu/litigation/cases/21cv08812.html

This litigation directly applies the deception gap this investigation documented
to the legal framework of securities fraud. It is a primary source that
independently corroborates the deception chronology.

Confidence in finding: 0.85
Recommendation: Open dedicated rabbit hole investigation into the securities
litigation. This is the documented legal mechanism through which the
