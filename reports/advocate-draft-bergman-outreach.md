Dear Mr. Bergman,

My name is Norm Robichaud. I'm the director of the Hoffman Lenses Initiative,
an independent research project building tools to make online behavioral
manipulation visible and documentable.

I'm writing because your work represents the most significant legal effort
to hold platforms accountable for harm to children, and I believe we have
something directly relevant to that effort.

**What we've built:**

The Hoffman Browser is an open-source desktop browser with on-device
manipulation detection. When a user visits a page, a local language model
reads the rendered text and identifies patterns associated with behavioral
manipulation — outrage engineering, false urgency, suppression framing,
tribal activation, and others. Critically, the analysis happens entirely
on the user's device. Nothing is sent to any server. The browser creates
a documented, reproducible record of what manipulation techniques were
present on a given page at a given time.

Alongside the browser, we're building the Behavioral Manipulation Intelligence
Database (BMID): an evidence-based intelligence repository documenting the
manipulation ecosystem. Every claim in the BMID requires primary source
documentation — court filings, sworn testimony, internal documents disclosed
through legal proceedings. It is structured for the standard of scrutiny
your work requires.

**Why I think this matters for your litigation:**

1. The browser produces a documented record of manipulation patterns
   on specific pages. That record is reproducible and time-stamped.

2. The BMID cross-references known operator techniques against
   what's detected — with source citations. It is designed to be
   citable.

3. Both tools are open source and built to run on any researcher's
   or attorney's machine without sending data anywhere.

I'm not seeking a financial arrangement. I'm seeking to understand whether
these tools would be useful to your team, and if so, to make them available
to you.
