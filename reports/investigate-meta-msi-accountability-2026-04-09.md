This report documents the **Meaningful Social Interactions (MSI) accountability chain** for
Meta Platforms, focusing on the gap between internal knowledge of harm and public characterization

**The core accountability finding:**

Q1 2020 and Q2 2021 — after the internal harm findings existed.

This is documented via primary sources available without litigation discovery.

## PART 1 — THE MSI ALGORITHM: WHAT IT IS AND WHAT IT DID

### Background

In January 2018, Meta deployed the Meaningful Social Interactions (MSI) update to the
Facebook News Feed algorithm. The stated purpose was to reduce passive content consumption
and increase "meaningful" interactions — comments, reactions, shares between friends and
family — as opposed to passive video watching.

**The mechanism:** MSI re-weighted the News Feed ranking algorithm to give heavier weight to
content that generated comments and reactions, versus content that was merely viewed or liked.

**The structural problem (documented internally by 2019):**
Outrage, fear, and divisive content systematically generate more comments and reactions than
neutral or affirming content. By re-weighting on engagement signals, MSI created an
amplification pathway that disproportionately elevated emotionally provocative content.

This is not speculation — it is documented in:
1. Frances Haugen's Senate testimony (October 5, 2021, sworn)
2. Internal Meta documents disclosed via Haugen and subsequent litigation
3. The WSJ Facebook Files (September–October 2021)
4. Independent academic research predating and corroborating MSI's mechanism

## PART 2 — THE SCIENCE OF MSI'S AMPLIFICATION MECHANISM

### Brady et al. (2017) — The mechanism documented before MSI

**Citation:** Brady, W.J., Wills, J.A., Jost, J.T., Tucker, J.A., Van Bavel, J.J. (2017).
Emotion shapes the diffusion of moralized content in social networks.
*Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 114(28), 7313–7318.
DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1618923114

**Key finding:** Each moral-emotional word in a social media post increases the probability
of sharing/retweeting by approximately **20%**.

**Why this matters for MSI:**
MSI weighted content by engagement signals (shares, comments, reactions).
Brady et al. documented — one year before MSI launched — that moral-emotional content
generates more engagement signals. When MSI went live, it was mechanically selecting for
exactly the content type Brady et al. had shown was most viral: moralized, emotional content.

**BMID significance:** This paper establishes that the amplification effect of engagement-
weighted algorithms on emotional content was documented scientific knowledge before Meta
deployed MSI. The company cannot credibly claim ignorance of the mechanism.

---

### Braghieri, Levy, Makarin (2022) — Causal harm, peer-reviewed

**Citation:** Braghieri, L., Levy, R., Makarin, A. (2022). Social Media and Mental Health.
*American Economic Review*, 112(11), 3660–3693.
DOI: 10.1257/aer.20211218

**Methodology:** Natural experiment. Facebook rolled out to U.S. college campuses in
staggered waves between 2004 and 2006. Authors use this variation as an instrument to
identify causal effects of Facebook access on mental health outcomes (using data from the
National College Health Assessment).

**Key finding:** Facebook access caused a **measurable, statistically significant
deterioration in student mental health.** This is a causal finding, not a correlation —
the natural experiment design accounts for selection effects.

**Published in:** The *American Economic Review* — the flagship journal of the American
Economic Association, one of the highest-prestige publication venues in economics.

**BMID significance:** This is the strongest academic evidence of Facebook's causal harm.
Published in 2022, it postdates MSI and provides independent corroboration of the harm
mechanism Haugen identified internally.

### What Haugen Documented

Frances Haugen was a data scientist at Meta from June 2019 to May 2021.
She served on Meta's Civic Integrity team.

**Senate testimony, October 5, 2021 (sworn):**
Source: U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation
URL: https://www.commerce.senate.gov/2021/10/protecting-kids-online-testimony-from-a-facebook-whistleblower

Key statements (from public transcript):

> "Facebook knows that they are leading to increased division, depression, and even physical
> violence. They know. And they have chosen to do nothing."

> "The company's leadership knows how to make Facebook and Instagram safer but won't make
> the necessary changes because they have put their astronomical profits before people."

On MSI specifically, Haugen testified that internal research found the algorithm caused
the platform to amplify **angry and outrage-generating content** because such content
received more comments — exactly the engagement signal MSI optimized for.

**Confidence:** 0.95 — sworn Senate testimony, public record, not disputed by Meta as
to the existence of the internal research (Meta disputed characterizations, not the
underlying documents' existence).

## PART 4 — THE DECEPTION RECORD: SEC EDGAR PATH B FINDINGS

### The Method

Meta Platforms (CIK: 0001326801) is required to file Form 8-K with the SEC following
each quarterly earnings release. These filings include the full earnings call transcript
as Exhibit 99.1. Earnings call statements by named executives, made in conjunction with
quarterly financial disclosure, are the most consistently available primary source for
executive public characterizations.

All filings are publicly available at:
https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0001326801&type=8-K

### Timeline: Internal Findings vs. Public Statements

**2018 Q1:** MSI algorithm deployed.
**2019:** Internal Meta research documents that MSI amplifies harmful, divisive, and
  outrage-generating content. Exact date of completion: not publicly confirmed.
  Haugen testimony places awareness "by 2019."
**2019–2021:** Internal proposals to fix MSI's harm effects considered but not
  implemented at scale (per Haugen testimony and WSJ Facebook Files).

## PART 7 — RABBIT HOLE FINDING: THE SOX ANGLE

**Finding:** Meta's earnings call statements are made in the context of Sarbanes-Oxley
Act (SOX) quarterly disclosures. SOX requires that executives certify the accuracy of
financial statements and material disclosures. If executives knew internal research
contradicted their public characterizations of MSI as a wellbeing improvement, and
those characterizations were made as part of SEC filings, there is a potential
securities disclosure question.

**Status:** This is a legal theory requiring counsel review. Flag for Director.

**Why it matters for BMID:** The SOX angle tightens the "knowing" element. Executives
don't make earnings call statements carelessly — they are prepared by legal and PR teams,
reviewed before delivery. The public characterizations of MSI as "healthier" were not
off-the-cuff remarks. They were prepared public statements made in conjunction with
SEC disclosure.

**Recommended action:** Flag for legal counsel review. Do not record as BMID fact.
Record as open legal question with citation to the relevant earnings calls.
