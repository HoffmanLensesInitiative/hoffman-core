## THE 2018 NEWS FEED ALGORITHM CHANGE — "MEANINGFUL SOCIAL INTERACTIONS"

### What Meta said publicly

On January 11, 2018, Mark Zuckerberg posted the following to his personal Facebook profile (public post, archived):

> "As we roll out this update, Pages may see their reach, video watch time and referral
> traffic decrease. The change will apply to all Pages, but [we] expect the biggest
> decreases for Pages whose posts prompt lots of comments and reactions but that people
> say are not meaningful to them."

Simultaneously, Facebook's Newsroom published an official announcement titled:
**"Bringing People Closer Together"**
Published: January 11, 2018
URL: https://about.fb.com/news/2018/01/news-feed-fyi-bringing-people-closer-together/
Author: Adam Mosseri (then VP, News Feed Product)

Key public claims in that announcement:
- "The goal of News Feed is to show people the stories that are most relevant to them."
- "We updated our survey so it asks people to rate posts as worth their time."
- "We want News Feed to be about the people and things that matter most to you."

Zuckerberg's January 11 post added:
> "The research shows that when we use social media to connect with people we care about,
> it can be good for our well-being. We can feel more connected and less lonely, and that
> correlates with long term measures of happiness and health."

**The framing was explicit:** this algorithm change was presented as a health and wellbeing
improvement, grounded in research, designed to prioritize meaningful connections over
passive consumption.

## WHAT INTERNAL RESEARCH SHOWED

### The "MSI" research findings (disclosed through Frances Haugen)

Frances Haugen, a former Facebook data scientist in the Civic Integrity team, disclosed
thousands of internal Facebook documents to the SEC and to journalists in 2021. The
documents were published in part by the Wall Street Journal (Facebook Files, September-
October 2021) and later by a consortium of 17 news organizations (the "Facebook Papers,"
October 2021).

**Primary source:**
Wall Street Journal — "The Facebook Files"
Lead reporters: Jeff Horwitz, Deepa Seetharaman, Georgia Wells, Rebecca Elliott
Published: September 14 – November 2021 (multi-part series)
URL: https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-facebook-files-11631713039

**What the disclosed documents showed regarding MSI:**

1. **Internal research showed MSI increased "angry" reactions disproportionately.**
   An internal presentation (cited in WSJ reporting, September 2021) showed that the
   algorithm change designed to promote "meaningful social interactions" in practice
   disproportionately amplified content that generated angry reactions (the "angry" emoji
   engagement was weighted 5x more than "likes" in the ranking signals, per disclosed
   documents). Outrage-generating content was systematically surfaced over other content
   because outrage drives comments — which the algorithm treated as evidence of
   "meaningful" interaction.

2. **A 2019 internal report flagged the MSI change as making political polarization worse.**
   Researchers in Facebook's Core Data Science team found that the MSI algorithm
   increased the proportion of politically divisive content in News Feeds. The report
   recommended reducing the weight of "reshares" in the ranking signal, finding that
   reshared content was disproportionately politically extreme. This recommendation
   was not implemented at scale. (WSJ, October 2021; Facebook Papers consortium)

3. **The angry emoji amplification was internally known and documented.**
   A 2020 internal presentation, disclosed through Haugen, showed that Facebook
   researchers had identified that the 5x weighting of the angry emoji in engagement
   scoring was amplifying inflammatory content and recommended removal of the weighting.
   Adam Mosseri and other executives were briefed. The 5x weighting was removed in
   2019 but other engagement amplification mechanics remained. (WSJ Facebook Files)

**Primary source — Frances Haugen Senate testimony:**
Title: "Protecting Kids Online: Testimony from a Facebook Whistleblower"
Committee: Senate Commerce Subcommittee on Consumer Protection
Date: October 5, 2021
Transcript URL: https://www.commerce.senate.gov/2021/10/protecting-kids-online
Direct quote (Haugen, sworn testimony):
> "Facebook's own research shows that it amplifies hatred, misinformation and political
> unrest, but the company hides what is happening on its platform."

> "The company intentionally hides vital information from the public, from the U.S.
> government, and from governments around the world."

## STATE ATTORNEYS GENERAL v. META — 2023

**This is a new primary source thread opened this cycle.**

On October 24, 2023, attorneys general from 41 states and the District of Columbia
filed a federal lawsuit against Meta Platforms, Inc.

**Filing:** State of New Mexico et al. v. Meta Platforms, Inc.
**Court:** United States District Court — Northern District of California
**Case number:** 4:23-cv-05448
**Filed:** October 24, 2023
**URL:** https://oag.ca.gov/system/files/media/meta-complaint-2023-10-24.pdf

**Relevance to MSI thread:**
The complaint cites internal Meta research, including research showing that Instagram
and Facebook features caused measurable harm to young users — and that Meta's
response was to continue or expand those features. The complaint also cites the
MSI algorithm's engagement optimization as contributing to harmful content exposure.

Key allegation (paragraph citations from complaint):
- Meta's own research showed that certain recommendation features caused teen users
  to receive harmful content at higher rates than adult users.
- Meta continued operating these features after receiving that research.
- This is the "knowing" element: documented internal research → continued operation.

**Confidence on complaint filing:** 1.0 (public court document)
**Confidence on complaint allegations:** 0.65 (allegations, not adjudicated findings)
Note: Allegations in a complaint are not proven facts. Record accordingly.

## THE UK THREAD — MOLLY RUSSELL INQUEST CONNECTION

The Molly Russell inquest (September 2022) is directly connected to the MSI thread
because Meta's algorithm decisions determined what content Instagram served to Molly
Russell in the months before her death.

**Primary source:**
HM Senior Coroner Andrew Walker
Inquest into the death of Molly Isabella Russell
Prevention of Future Deaths report issued: October 2022
Relevant finding: Instagram's recommendation algorithm served self-harm and suicide
content to a 14-year-old user without meaningful intervention.

The MSI algorithm (News Feed) and Instagram's recommendation algorithm share
structural similarities — both optimize for engagement rather than wellbeing.
The Instagram teen harm research (documented in prior cycles) shows this was
internally known.

**Rabbit hole flag:** The question of whether the engagement-optimization architecture
developed for Facebook News Feed was ported to Instagram and with what internal
documentation of harm — this is a tractable research thread. Instagram was acquired
in 2012; the MSI change came in 2018. What did internal researchers document about
Instagram's algorithm changes in the same period?

## RABBIT HOLE FINDINGS

### RABBIT HOLE 1: The Angry Emoji Weighting Architecture

The 5x weighting of the angry emoji in Facebook's engagement scoring is a
specific, documented, testable design decision that directly caused outrage
amplification. This is not an emergent side effect — it was an explicit
product design choice with documented internal debate about its effects.

The question this opens: **who made the specific product decision to weight
angry engagement at 5x?** Was this documented in product specifications?
Who approved it? This is traceable to a named individual or team.

Sources to pursue: Internal product specifications (via litigation discovery),
Engineering team attribution, Former employees who may have testified or
published post-employment.

### RABBIT HOLE 2: The 2019 "Polarization" Research Suppression Thread

Internal Meta researchers recommended reducing reshare weighting due to polarization
effects in 2019. The recommendation was not implemented at scale. The question is:
**who made the decision not to implement this recommendation, and what was the
documented reason?**

If the reason was revenue protection — that implementing the recommendation would
reduce engagement and therefore ad revenue — that is documented knowing prioritization
of profit over documented harm reduction. This is a high-value legal thread.

Sources to pursue: Facebook Papers primary documents, litigation discovery,
former researcher accounts (multiple former Meta researchers have published
or testified post-employment).

### RABBIT HOLE 3: The Systemic Pattern Across Platforms

The MSI algorithm change at Facebook, the recommendation algorithm changes at
YouTube (documented in prior academic research as enabling radicalization pathways),
and TikTok's "For You" algorithm (under Senate investigation) all show the same
structural pattern: engagement optimization → outrage/fear amplification →
documented harm → internal knowledge → continued operation.

This is not coincidence. It may reflect:
a) Independent convergent optimization toward the same locally-optimal-but-harmful design
b) Copying between platforms (Meta and others have been documented watching each other)
c) Shared investor pressure for engagement metrics

This cross-platform structural pattern belongs in the BMID network layer.
It may be legally significant as evidence of industry-wide knowing practice.
