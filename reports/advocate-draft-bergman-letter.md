---

**What we have built**

The Hoffman Lenses Initiative has developed two tools that may be directly useful to your litigation work:

**1. The Hoffman Browser**
A Chromium-based desktop browser with on-device manipulation detection built in. It reads the rendered text of any web page — not the underlying code — and uses a local AI model to identify manipulation techniques: outrage engineering, false urgency, tribal activation, suppression framing, and others. The analysis runs entirely on-device. No data leaves the machine.

For litigation purposes: the browser can document, in real time, what manipulation techniques are present in the content a user is being served. It produces structured output — pattern type, confidence score, explanation, evidence. That output is reproducible and can be generated on demand for any URL.

**2. The Behavioral Manipulation Intelligence Database (BMID)**
An open intelligence repository documenting the supply chain of online manipulation: who operates these systems, how they monetize them, what harm has resulted, and what those operators knew and when they knew it.

The BMID is built on an evidence integrity standard that should feel familiar to litigators: every entry requires primary source documentation — court filings, sworn testimony, regulatory filings, whistleblower disclosures, named journalism. Unknown is recorded as unknown. Evidence gaps are investigation targets, not reasons to infer.

Current records include documented harm findings from the Molly Russell UK inquest (2022), congressional testimony, and the Facebook Papers. We are actively expanding to cover YouTube, TikTok, and additional Meta properties.

---

**Why I am writing to you specifically**

The Dominion case demonstrated that internal communications are the decisive evidence in platform accountability litigation. The Facebook Papers, the Frances Haugen disclosures, the Molly Russell inquest — all turned on the question: what did they know, and when?

The BMID is being built specifically to map that question across operators. For every major platform, we are documenting: the individual actors with decision-making authority, the moments they received internal evidence of harm, what they said publicly after receiving that evidence, and what actions they did or did not take.

This is not advocacy research. It is structured, evidence-sourced documentation built to withstand scrutiny in exactly the settings where your work happens.

If any portion of what we are building — the detection tool, the intelligence database, the actor accountability records — could serve your clients or your investigators, I want to make it available.

---

**What I am asking**

I am not asking for anything in this letter.

I am introducing the project, offering the tools, and asking whether a conversation would be useful. If there is a researcher on your team, a paralegal, an investigator — anyone who might benefit from a demonstration of the browser or a walk through the BMID structure — I am available.

I am also open to understanding what documentation your cases most need that we are not yet producing. The BMID is a living database. Its research priorities are shaped by what will actually serve accountability.

## DIRECTOR REVIEW NOTES

**Flags for Norm's attention before sending:**

1. **Tone check** — This letter is direct and substantive. It does not lead with emotional appeal or ask for anything. That is intentional — litigators respond to tools and evidence, not sympathy. But Norm should confirm this register matches his voice and intent.

2. **Capability accuracy** — The letter describes the Hoffman Browser and BMID as they are documented in HOFFMAN.md. However, Norm should verify that the browser's output is currently reproducible and structured enough to be characterized as "reproducible on demand for any URL." If it is not yet at that level of reliability, soften that claim before sending.

3. **BMID evidence completeness** — The letter says the BMID documents "what those operators knew and when they knew it" for major platforms. Confirm current BMID coverage is sufficient to support that characterization before sending, or narrow the claim to what is currently documented (Meta / Facebook specifically).

4. **The "not asking for anything" framing** — This is deliberate. Cold outreach to a senior litigation attorney that opens with an ask is likely to be ignored. This letter offers and introduces. But if Norm prefers to include a specific ask (e.g., a 30-minute call, a specific case reference), that can be added to the closing paragraph.

5. **Email infrastructure prerequisite** — This letter cannot be sent until legal@hoffmanlenses.org is live via Proton Mail. Do not send from a personal address. The domain matters for credibility.

6. **Timing** — Consider whether to send this before or after the Hoffman Browser has OCR capability. The OCR integration (currently in development) would strengthen the capability description. However, waiting indefinitely is also a cost. Norm's call.

7. **The Blackwell paragraph** — Ending with JackLynn is intentional and true. Some recipients will find it powerful. A small number may find it manipulative to deploy a child's name in a professional introduction. I believe it is appropriate here — the families are the reason this project exists, and Bergman represents families — but Norm should read it with that question in mind.
