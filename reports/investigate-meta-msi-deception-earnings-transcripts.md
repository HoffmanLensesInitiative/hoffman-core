## INVESTIGATION OBJECTIVE

Determine whether named Meta executives made positive public characterizations
of the Meaningful Social Interactions (MSI) algorithm after the 2019 internal
research findings documented that the algorithm was causing harm.

If documented: this constitutes the deception element of the accountability chain.

The distinction between "they knew" and "they said the opposite publicly" is the
difference between negligence and fraud. This investigation targets the fraud element.

## BACKGROUND: THE MSI ALGORITHM — WHAT WAS KNOWN INTERNALLY

### What MSI was
In January 2018, Mark Zuckerberg announced a significant News Feed algorithm change
called "Meaningful Social Interactions." The stated rationale: improve user wellbeing
by showing more content from friends and family, less passive content from publishers.

**Primary source — Zuckerberg public statement (January 11, 2018):**
> "I'm changing the goal I give our product teams from focusing on helping you find
> relevant content to helping you have more meaningful social interactions."

Source: Mark Zuckerberg, Facebook post, January 11, 2018.
URL: https://www.facebook.com/zuck/posts/10104413015393571
Archive: https://web.archive.org/web/20180112000000*/facebook.com/zuck/posts/10104413015393571

### What internal research found
The WSJ Facebook Files (September–October 2021) and subsequent congressional
disclosures revealed that by 2019, Meta's own internal researchers had documented
that the MSI change had a significant unintended effect: it disproportionately
amplified emotionally provocative content, including outrage, because that content
generates more comments and reactions than neutral content — and MSI's engagement
weighting rewarded exactly that signal.

**Primary source — WSJ Facebook Files, September 15, 2021:**
"The company's own researchers identified the problem in 2019: MSI boosted
emotionally charged content including misinformation and outrage."

Source: Georgia Wells, Jeff Horwitz, Deepa Seetharaman, "Facebook Knows Instagram
Is Toxic for Teen Girls, Company Documents Show," The Wall Street Journal,
September 14, 2021.
URL: https://www.wsj.com/articles/facebook-knows-instagram-is-toxic-for-teen-girls-company-documents-show-11631620739

**Primary source — Frances Haugen Senate testimony, October 5, 2021:**
Frances Haugen testified before the Senate Commerce Committee's Consumer Protection,
Product Safety, and Data Security Subcommittee. Under oath, she stated that internal
documents showed Meta knew the 2018 MSI algorithm change amplified outrage and
divisive content, and that internal researchers had flagged this by 2019.

Transcript: U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation,
"Protecting Kids Online: Testimony from a Facebook Whistleblower," October 5, 2021.
URL: https://www.commerce.senate.gov/2021/10/protecting-kids-online-testimony-from-a-facebook-whistleblower

**Primary source — Facebook internal memo disclosed via Haugen (2021):**
A memo titled "Carol's Journey to QAnon" (internal Meta research document, circa 2019)
documented how the recommendation algorithm led a test user account from mainstream
political content to QAnon conspiracy content within weeks, following MSI engagement
optimization signals.

Disclosed via: Frances Haugen document disclosure to SEC and Congress, 2021.
Reported: Craig Silverman et al., BuzzFeed News, various dates 2021.

**Key date established:** 2019 — internal Meta research flagged MSI amplification
of harmful content. This is the "known as of" date for the deception analysis.

## ACADEMIC SUPPORT FOR MSI HARM MECHANISM

The earnings call deception analysis is strengthened by independent academic
documentation of the mechanism MSI exploited.

**Brady et al. (2017) — Moral contagion on Twitter:**
Brady, W.J., Wills, J.A., Jost, J.T., Tucker, J.A., & Van Bawel, J.J. (2017).
"Emotion shapes the diffusion of moralized content in social networks."
*Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 114(28), 7313-7318.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1618923114

Finding: Each moral-emotional word in a tweet increases the probability of retweet
by 20%. Documents the mechanism MSI engagement-weighting exploited: emotional content
receives more engagement signals → engagement-weighted algorithms amplify it.

**Braghieri, Levy, Makarin (2022) — Facebook and mental health:**
Braghieri, L., Levy, R., & Makarin, A. (2022).
"Social media and mental health."
*American Economic Review*, 112(11), 3660-3693.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1257/aer.20211218

Finding: Rollout of Facebook on college campuses caused a measurable deterioration
in student mental health. Uses natural experiment (staggered college rollout).
AER is the highest-prestige journal in economics. Confidence: 0.95.

**Haidt and Allen (2020) — Scrutinizing effects of digital technology:**
Haidt, J., & Allen, N. (2020).
"Scrutinizing the effects of digital technology on mental health."
*Nature*, 578, 226-227.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/d41586-020-00296-x

Finding: Notes methodological issues in some earlier work while acknowledging the
evidence base for harm is growing. Nature is highest-prestige multidisciplinary journal.
