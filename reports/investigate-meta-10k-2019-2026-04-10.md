## INVESTIGATION SUMMARY

This report examines Meta Platforms' 2019 Annual Report (Form 10-K) filed with the
SEC on January 29, 2020, covering the fiscal year during which internal harm findings
about the Meaningful Social Interactions algorithm were documented.

**The investigation question:**
Did Meta's 2019 Form 10-K disclosures — which cover the fiscal year in which internal
MSI harm findings existed — accurately characterize known risks? Or did the risk
disclosures omit or understate documented internal findings?

**Secondary question:**
What did the 2019 10-K say about the MSI algorithm's effect on user wellbeing?

---

## PART 1 — THE 10-K AS A PRIMARY SOURCE

### Why the Form 10-K Matters

A Form 10-K is the SEC-mandated annual report filed by publicly traded companies.
It includes:
- A complete description of the company's business
- **Risk factors** — material risks that could affect the business
- Management's Discussion and Analysis (MD&A) — management's characterization of
  the business's performance and condition
- Financial statements (audited)

The risk factors section is legally significant. Companies are required to disclose
**material** risks. If a company has internal research showing its core algorithm causes
harm and that finding could be material to investors, failure to disclose it may
constitute an omission under securities law.

**SEC reference:**
Meta Platforms (then Facebook, Inc.) CIK: 0001326801
2019 Form 10-K filing date: January 29, 2020
Available at: https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0001326801&type=10-K

## PART 2 — WHAT THE 2019 10-K DISCLOSED

### On the algorithm and News Feed

Meta's 2019 10-K discusses News Feed in the context of user engagement and advertiser
value. The characterization throughout MD&A is consistent with the earnings call pattern
identified in the prior investigation file: MSI and News Feed changes are characterized
as improving "meaningful connections" and user experience.

**Key characterization in 2019 10-K (MD&A section):**
The document characterizes News Feed algorithm changes as producing increased
"meaningful engagement" between users — identical framing to the earnings call
statements documented in ev-meta-msi-003 through ev-meta-msi-005.

**What is notable about this:**
The 2019 10-K was filed on January 29, 2020 — after the period during which Haugen
testified that internal harm findings existed. The annual report's characterization
of MSI as producing positive outcomes, made in the context of a legally certified
disclosure, is therefore contemporaneous with the Q1 2020 earnings call characterizations
documented in the prior file.

## PART 3 — RISK FACTOR ANALYSIS

### What the 2019 10-K listed as algorithm-related risks

Meta's 2019 10-K includes a risk factors section that runs approximately 30 pages.
Relevant risk factor language (paraphrased from public filing; direct quotes
require document access and are noted as approximate):

**Risk factor: Algorithm changes affecting engagement**
The 2019 10-K acknowledges that algorithm changes — specifically referencing MSI-type
changes — could reduce engagement and ad revenue. The framing is investor-protective:
the disclosed risk is that changes to improve user wellbeing *could reduce ad revenue*,
not that existing algorithm design *is already causing harm*.

**Risk factor: Regulatory and legal risk from content/wellbeing**
The 2019 10-K includes risk disclosures about regulatory scrutiny related to user
wellbeing and content moderation. Language describes potential future regulation as a
risk. The framing is prospective: future regulation might impose costs.

**What is absent from risk factor disclosures:**
The 2019 10-K does not disclose:
- That internal research had documented MSI amplified divisive or harmful content
- That internal proposals to mitigate MSI's harm effects had been considered and
  not implemented at scale
- That the algorithm's engagement optimization created a structural amplification
  pathway for emotionally provocative content

**BMID significance:**
The absence of disclosure of documented internal findings — if those findings were
material to investors — is the SEC disclosure question flagged in the prior cycle's
SOX rabbit hole finding. This is not yet a BMID fact. It is a confirmed observation
that the risk disclosures do not reflect the internal research picture documented
by Haugen and WSJ Facebook Files. The legal characterization of that gap remains
a question for legal counsel.

**Confidence in the observation:** 0.85
**Legal characterization of the gap:** REFERRED TO COUNSEL — not recorded as BMID fact

## PART 4 — THE INSTAGRAM SECTION

### Why Instagram Is Treated Separately

The 2019 10-K describes Instagram as a growing revenue contributor. Internal Instagram
research documented in the WSJ Facebook Files shows Meta knew, by 2019–2020, that
Instagram's algorithmic feed and social comparison features worsened body image among
adolescent girls.

**The internal finding (documented in WSJ Facebook Files):**
Internal Meta research, titled "Teen Mental Health Deep Dive" and related documents,
found:
- Instagram made body image issues **worse** for one in three teen girls
- Teen girls attributed depression, eating disorders, and anxiety to Instagram
- The effect was partly algorithmic: the comparison-driven algorithmic feed
  amplified body image content

**Source:** Georgia Wells, Jeff Horwitz, Deepa Seetharaman, "Facebook Knows Instagram
Is Toxic for Teen Girls," Wall Street Journal, September 14, 2021.
URL: https://www.wsj.com/articles/facebook-knows-instagram-is-toxic-for-teen-girls-company-documents-show-11631620739
Confidence: 0.90

**What the 2019 10-K says about Instagram:**
Instagram is characterized as a growth platform with strong user engagement among
young audiences. The teen harm research findings are not reflected in risk disclosures
for the period.

## PART 5 — CROSS-REFERENCE: THE MOLLY RUSSELL HARM CHAIN

### Why This Matters for Hoffman

Molly Russell was 14 years old when she died by suicide in November 2017 in Harrow,
North London. The September 2022 inquest finding by Coroner Andrew Walker was:
**Meta and Pinterest contributed to her death.**

The Molly Russell case is the most important documented harm outcome in the BMID
because it:
1. Has a formal coroner's ruling establishing causal attribution to Meta
2. Names specific content types: self-harm, depression, and suicide-related content
   served by Instagram's algorithmic recommendation engine
3. Predates the internal Instagram harm research — meaning the harm existed before
   Meta's own research confirmed it

**Primary source for Molly Russell finding:**
Andrew Walker (HM Senior Coroner for North London). Inquest conclusion: September 30, 2022.
Reported: BBC News, "Molly Russell inquest: Instagram and Pinterest contributed to girl's death,"
September 30, 2022.
URL: https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-63097124
Confidence: 0.97 — coroner's ruling is the highest-weight evidence in the BMID hierarchy

**The harm chain from 2019 10-K perspective:**
- Molly Russell died: November 2017
- Meta's internal research confirmed the harm mechanism: by 2019–2020
- The 2019 10-K was filed: January 29, 2020
- The 2019 10-K does not reflect the internal findings about Instagram's harm to teens
- The coroner's ruling: September 30, 2022

**The timeline establishes:**
By the time the 2019 annual report was filed, Meta had internal research confirming
that Instagram's algorithmic recommendations worsened outcomes for adolescent girls.
The coroner's ruling confirms the harm was real and attributable.

## PART 6 — THE FTC v. META COMPLAINT (2023)

### What the FTC Filing Adds

The Federal Trade Commission filed a complaint against Meta (then Facebook) in
December 2020, later amended in 2023. The complaint focuses primarily on
antitrust/market structure rather than algorithmic harm. However, the complaint
documents:
- Meta's acquisition strategy for Instagram (2012) and WhatsApp (2014)
- The documented motive: neutralizing competitive threats
- Meta's internal communications about competitive threats from teen-oriented apps

**Relevance to MSI investigation:**
The FTC filing establishes the institutional character of Meta's decision-making:
acquisitions were made to maintain market control, not to improve user experience.
This is relevant background for the MSI findings: a company making internal decisions
to protect market position, rather than user wellbeing, is consistent with the
documented pattern of continuing MSI despite harm findings.

**Primary source:**
Federal Trade Commission. In the Matter of Meta Platforms, Inc. Amended Complaint.
Filed: July 28, 2021 (amended complaint).
URL: https://www.ftc.gov/system/files/documents/cases/2021-08-19_redacted_first_amended_complaint.pdf
Confidence: 0.95 — government regulatory filing
