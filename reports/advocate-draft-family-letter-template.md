**Subject:** Thinking of [CHILD'S NAME]

Dear [FAMILY NAME],

I am writing to you with deep respect for your grief and hesitation about adding to the weight you carry.

My name is Norm Robichaud. I live in British Columbia, Canada, and I am building a project called Hoffman Lenses. It is a research tool designed to make visible the manipulation techniques that social media platforms use — techniques that, in too many cases, have led to outcomes no family should ever face.

I learned about [CHILD'S NAME] through [HOW — be specific: news coverage, court records, foundation website]. I do not pretend to understand what you have been through. I only know that [CHILD'S NAME] deserved better than to be treated as an engagement metric by systems designed without regard for human wellbeing.

I am not writing to ask anything of you. I am writing because I believe you should know this project exists, and because I want to offer whatever resources might be useful to you — on your terms, in your time, or not at all.

**What Hoffman Lenses is:**
A browser-based tool that identifies manipulation patterns in online content — the same patterns that platforms use to maximize engagement regardless of harm. It is a research instrument, not a consumer product. It is designed for families, researchers, journalists, and attorneys who want to understand and document what these systems do.

The project includes a white paper titled "The Algorithm and the Child: A Human Rights Case for Abolishing Behavioral Manipulation Systems." It documents, with evidence and legal citations, how platforms have known about harms to children and continued operating unchanged. A copy is attached if you wish to read it. If not, that is completely fine.

**What I can offer:**
- A copy of the white paper and any project materials
- Connection to the Social Media Victims Law Center, if you are not already in contact with them (they represent many families in litigation against these platforms)
- A place on our remembrance page at hoffmanlenses.org, if you would ever want [CHILD'S NAME] to be remembered there — only with your explicit consent, written in your words, removed at any time you wish
- Nothing at all, if that is what you prefer

**What I will never do:**
- Contact you again if you ask me not to
- Share your information with anyone
- Use [CHILD'S NAME]'s story publicly without your explicit permission
- Pressure you to participate in anything

If you would like to talk, I am available by email or phone at your convenience. If you would prefer I not contact you again, simply say so, and I will honor that completely.

I am sorry for your loss. [CHILD'S NAME] mattered. [HE/SHE/THEY] still [DOES/DO].

With respect,

Norm Robichaud
