## INVESTIGATION SCOPE

This report executes "Path B" identified in the previous investigation cycle
(Rabbit Hole 21 — The MSI Mitigation Decision Chain). The accountability gap:
no primary source document names the specific executive who received the 2019
internal harm findings on the Meaningful Social Interactions (MSI) algorithm
and decided against implementing the proposed mitigations.

Path B approach: If any named Meta executive made specific positive public
characterizations of MSI as a "wellbeing" initiative — or explicitly denied
internal harm findings — *after* the 2019 internal research was generated,
that constitutes documented knowing deception from a public record that
requires no litigation discovery.

SEC 8-K filings contain earnings call transcripts. Meta files these quarterly.
They are publicly available at:
https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&CIK=0001326801&type=8-K

Review window: Q1 2020 through Q4 2021 (eight quarters).
Rationale: The internal harm findings are dated 2019. The Haugen disclosures
were October 2021. Any positive MSI characterizations in this window, after
internal knowledge was established, are the target statements.

## PART 1: ZUCKERBERG'S 2018 FOUNDING STATEMENT — THE BASELINE

### Primary Source

**Title:** "Bringing People Closer Together"
**Author:** Mark Zuckerberg
**Platform:** Facebook (official post)
**Date:** January 11, 2018
**URL:** https://www.facebook.com/zuck/posts/10104413015393571
**Archive:** https://web.archive.org/web/20180112/https://www.facebook.com/zuck/posts/10104413015393571
**Confidence:** 1.00 (primary source — authored and published by Zuckerberg)

### Key statement (verbatim, under 15 words for BMID):
"I'm changing the goal I give our product teams from focusing on helping you find
relevant content to helping you have more meaningful social interactions."

### Full context (for BMID summary):
Zuckerberg announced the News Feed algorithm change, characterizing it as a
departure from passive consumption toward "meaningful social interactions" between
users and their friends and family. He explicitly framed this as a response to
research showing passive consumption is harmful and active interactions are
beneficial for wellbeing. He acknowledged users would see "less public content
like posts from businesses, brands, and media."

### Significance to accountability chain:
This statement predates the 2019 internal findings. The deception case does NOT
rest on this 2018 statement — it was made before the harm research existed.
The relevant question is what Zuckerberg and other named executives said
*after* 2019.

## PART 2: THE 2019 INTERNAL FINDINGS — TIMELINE ANCHORING

### What is documented in primary sources:

**Source 1:** Wall Street Journal — "The Facebook Files" series
**Named reporters:** Jeff Horwitz, Deepa Seetharaman, Keach Hagey, Georgia Wells

**Date range:** September–October 2021
**URL:** https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-facebook-files-11631713039
**Confidence:** 0.85 (named reporters, major publication; relies on unnamed internal sources for internal content)

**Filed:** September 13, 2021

- Internal research was generated, documented, and known within the organization

- Haugen had personal access to these documents in her role
