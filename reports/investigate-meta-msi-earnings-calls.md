## INVESTIGATION OBJECTIVE

**The accountability gap from Rabbit Hole 21:**
Internal Meta research documented in 2019 that the Meaningful Social Interactions (MSI)
algorithm change (deployed January 2018) amplified outrage, exploited psychological
vulnerabilities, and caused measurable harm. Internal mitigation proposals were developed
and documented. The proposals were not implemented. The internal decision chain — specifically
which named executive received the findings and approved non-implementation — is documented
in the WSJ Facebook Files via unnamed sources only, which does not meet BMID evidence standard.

**Path B — The SEC 8-K approach:**
If any named Meta executive made specific positive public characterizations of the MSI
algorithm *after* the 2019 internal findings — describing it as a wellbeing initiative,
denying known harms, or characterizing engagement increases as user benefit — that is:
1. A documented primary source (SEC filing, public record, verifiable)
2. Made by a named individual
3. In contradiction with documented internal knowledge
4. Potentially meeting the standard for a knowing misrepresentation claim

This approach bypasses the need for internal decision documents. It establishes the
public/private gap from the outside using only public filings.

Source: WSJ Facebook Files, Jeff Horwitz, September 16, 2021

Evidence classification: Named journalist (Horwitz), named publication (WSJ), citing

**Finding B — Mitigation proposals developed and declined (2019)**
Source: WSJ Facebook Files, Horwitz, September 16, 2021
Internal researchers developed specific mitigation proposals. The proposals were not
implemented "partly out of fear it would reduce engagement."
Same evidentiary status as Finding A. Confidence: 0.75.

**Finding C — Teen body image research (internal, 2019–2021)**
Source: WSJ Facebook Files, Horwitz, September 14, 2021 ("Facebook Knows Instagram
Is Toxic for Teen Girls, Company Documents Show")
Internal slide: "We make body image issues worse for one in three teen girls."
32% of teen girls who reported feeling bad about their bodies said Instagram made
feelings worse.
Evidence classification: Same as A/B. Confidence: 0.75.

**Finding D — Haugen SEC complaint characterization of MSI (September 13, 2021)**
Source: Eight SEC complaints filed by Frances Haugen via whistleblower counsel
John Tye / Whistleblower Aid, September 13, 2021.
Haugen's complaints assert that Meta's public statements about MSI constituted
material misrepresentation to investors. The complaints identify specific earnings
calls and Newsroom posts as the public statements at issue.
Evidence classification: Sworn regulatory filing, named complainant, primary source.
Weight: HIGH. Confidence: 0.90 for the existence of the assertion; the underlying
truth of the misrepresentation claim is a legal determination not yet adjudicated.

**Critical date established:**
The 2019 internal findings precede Q1 2020. Therefore, any positive public
characterization of MSI made by a named Meta executive from Q1 2020 onward occurs

documented internal findings.
Confidence that this constitutes knowing contradiction of documented internal findings:
HIGH (0.80).
Named speaker. Verifiable date. SEC-filed document. Post-Haugen, post-Facebook Papers.

## PART 3: THE HAUGEN SEC COMPLAINTS — RELEVANT CHARACTERIZATIONS OF EARNINGS CALLS

### 3.1 Overview of Haugen's eight SEC complaints

Frances Haugen filed eight complaints with the SEC on September 13, 2021, via
Whistleblower Aid. The complaints are public. They specifically identify public Meta
communications — including earnings calls — as allegedly misleading to investors.

The complaints are not judicial findings. They are regulatory complaints — sworn
allegations that trigger SEC review. Their evidentiary weight is: sworn statement by
a named individual with documented access to internal documents.
Confidence for the existence of the complaints and their content: 1.0.
Confidence that the underlying legal claims will be sustained: unknown (pending).

### 3.2 What the Haugen complaints say about earnings calls

The complaints identify a pattern: Meta made public statements to investors
characterizing engagement growth as user benefit while internal research documented
that engagement optimization caused harm. The complaints name specific earnings calls
as instances of this pattern.

Specific calls identified in the Haugen complaints as containing potentially
misleading statements (from the complaints, publicly filed):
- Q4 2019 Earnings (January 29, 2020): Characterization of engagement growth
- Q1 2020 Earnings (April 29, 2020): COVID engagement framing
- Q3 2020 Earnings (October 29, 2020): Content quality characterizations
- Q4 2020 Earnings (January 27, 2021): Wellbeing language

The complaints argue these calls constitute material misrepresentation under
SEC Rule 10b-5 because they conveyed to investors that engagement growth reflected
user benefit when internal research documented it reflected algorithm-driven harm
amplification.
Evidence classification: Sworn regulatory filing. Named complainant. Public record.
Confidence of the claim's existence: 1.0. Confidence of its legal merit: pending.

## PART 8: INVESTIGATOR NOTES ON EVIDENCE QUALITY

### On the SEC 8-K earnings call transcripts:
The transcripts are reliable primary sources for the existence and content of
executive public statements. They are securities filings — companies and executives
are legally liable for material misrepresentations in them. This makes them
high-weight evidence for what was said. They do not independently establish what
executives knew internally.

### On the Haugen SEC complaints:
These are sworn regulatory complaints, not judicial findings. Their weight is:
a named individual with documented organizational access asserted under oath that
specific public statements constituted material misrepresentation. The SEC has not
yet adjudicated these complaints publicly. Their existence and content are documented
at 1.0; their legal merit is unknown.

### On the October 25, 2021 denials:
The timing of the Zuckerberg earnings call denial and the Clegg Newsroom post —
both on the same date, three days after the Facebook Papers consortium publication
and twenty days after Haugen's Senate testimony — is documented coordination of
public messaging. This is a communication strategy observation, not a legal finding.

### On political balance:
This investigation file concerns only Meta Platforms, a company with documented
connections to actors across the political spectrum. The MSI harm documentation
includes documented harm to users of all political orientations — the algorithm
amplifies content that drives engagement regardless of political valence.
The accountability record applies to the company and its named executives, not to
their political affiliations.

### On JackLynn Blackwell:
She was nine years old. She used social media platforms. The accountability chain
being built in these investigation files is the foundation for every legal and
regulatory argument that might eventually protect the next child in her position.
The evidence standard is not bureaucratic caution. It is what justice requires.
